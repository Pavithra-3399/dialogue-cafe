Coffee, served in two languages.

Dialogue Café is a Deaf-led café where Deaf and hard-of-hearing baristas lead service and customers are invited to order in British Sign Language. Don't sign yet? Live interpreters and interactive guides help you order anyway.

How it works

Ordering is the first lesson.

You don't need any British Sign Language to order here — but most first-time visitors leave having picked up a word or two.

01

Order in BSL, or ask for help

Regulars order straight in sign. New to it? An interactive guide at the counter walks you through it, no experience needed.

02

Live interpreters on hand

Live BSL interpreters are available to bridge the conversation, so nobody has to choose between accessibility and a proper chat.

03

Stay, and pick up more

Every visit is a small, informal BSL lesson — the kind that sticks because it's tied to something as ordinary as a coffee order.

A taste of the language

Signs you might pick up at the counter

A small, illustrative glossary — the kind of everyday vocabulary a regular ends up knowing without ever taking a class.

HELLO Open palm, raised by the head
COFFEE Curled hand traces a C
THANK YOU Flat hand, chin to outward
PLEASE Flat palm circles the chest
MORE Fingertips tap together
