Our story

Inclusion, not awareness.

Dialogue Café is the flagship café of Dialogue Hub CIC — a Deaf-led social enterprise founded in 2019 by Hakan Elbir, built on the idea that lived inclusion works better than awareness campaigns.

The mission

Creating connection through coffee, BSL and inclusive experiences

That's Dialogue Hub's own mission statement, and it's built into how the café runs day to day: Deaf and hard-of-hearing baristas in visible, paid roles — not back-of-house — with BSL learning happening through ordinary, everyday interaction rather than a scheduled "awareness session."

The café opened within the Royal Docks Centre for Sustainability at UEL's Docklands Campus in early 2025, making its public debut at the Newham Chamber of Commerce Big Budget Breakfast on 19 February 2025.

Who's behind the counter

Employment first, everything else follows

Five new paid roles were created at this café for Deaf and hard-of-hearing staff when it opened. Alongside them, UEL students are trained and employed as baristas and interns, working shoulder to shoulder with the Deaf-led team.

  • Deaf and hard-of-hearing baristas lead service and set the pace of every shift.
  • UEL students train and work here as baristas and interns.
  • Live BSL interpreters and interactive ordering guides support anyone who hasn't signed before.
